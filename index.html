Cancer Analysis Research

Vision & Image Processing Lab
University of Waterloo

In my research, I worked on lung cancer analysis through CT scans. Using MATLAB, I implemented a polar coordinate based edge detection algorithm. Some initial image processing was required to select and clean the CT scan from an array of scans. In traditional techniques, a healthcare professional must create a click-driver polygon to isolate the tumor. Using the single click algorithm my team developed, the same result is produced with perfect accuracy in a very fast time. This automated process allows for quicker cancer analysis. The polar coordinate system was used to create “bins” that originate from the single click inside the tumor. Then, using statistical analysis, “bins” can be compared to isolate the tumor. The project was completed in collaboration with Sunnybrook Research Institute in Toronto, Canada.

In my second research project with the Vision & Image Processing Lab, I created a graphic user interface using MATLAB GUIDE to process prostate cancer MRIs. The interface allowed one to process raw MRI scans and execute multiple algorithms on them.

  • Result: An abstract was published at the ImNO (Imaging Network of Ontario) 2016 conference. The presentation was awarded a Cum Laude distinction. In addition, I was awarded the President’s Research Award twice for my contributions.

  • Date: September 2016 to April 2017, September 2015 to August 2016

  • Technical Skills: MATLAB, GUI, Image Processing, Research, Statistics, Algorithms

  • Category: Research Experience - Healthcare

Bridge Design

Project Manager
University of Waterloo

Using numerical methods, a 2D truss solver was developed in MATLAB. This was used to quickly evaluate different bridge designs. In the next stage, CAD models were created in SOLIDWORKS for laser cutting parts from balsa wood. Wood samples were tested for accurate material properties. These properties and models were used in ANSYS AIM for stress and deformation analysis. In order to optimize the design, a 3D surface was created by analyzing the effect of the thickness of 2 key bridge pieces on the resulting performance grade. The final bridge was designed for the performance metric that rewarded offset loading.

  • Result: The final bridge received a 100% score on the performance metric.

  • Date: May to August 2016

  • Technical Skills: Rapid Prototyping, Material Characterization, SOLIDWORKS, ANSYS AIM, Testing, Laser Cutting
